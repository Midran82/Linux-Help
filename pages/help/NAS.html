NAS on Linux

Info

The thing I was struggling most with was how to mount my NAS folders onto the Linux system. But after a while I found out that the best and easiest way to do it was to just do it simple and straight forward, So to make it easy for you guys, just follow these instructions.
This work with Ubuntu Desktop/Server and Linux Mint (might also work on other Linux OS).

Installation

Step 1: Open Terminal window.
Step 2: Install cifs-utils on your system:
sudo apt-get install cifs-utils.
Step 3: Make the folder you want the NAS to mount into. I always mount it into the /media folder:
cd /media : sudo mkdir mnt (or what you want to call your folder).
Step 4: Mount the storage:
sudo mount -t cifs //NAS_address/NAS_folder -o username=NAS_username,password=NAS_password,rw,nounix,isocharset=utf8, file_mode=0644,dir_mode=0755 /media/your_folder
Step 5: To mount the NAS on reboot/startup:
sudo nano /etc/fstab
then insert this code into the end:
//NAS_address/NAS_folder /media/your_folder cifs default,username=NAS_username,password=NAS_password 0 0
***You need to change NAS_address, your_folder, NAS_username and NAS_password into what your system has.***
